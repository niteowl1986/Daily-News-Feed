Co-Borrower Shares Similar And Equal Responsibility Under Loan Agreement: NCLAT New Delhi - Live Law - Indian Legal News

Published Date: Sun, 06 Oct 2024

The National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) has ruled that a coborrower is equally and severally liable under a loan agreement with that of the primary borrower. In the case of Amit Narang v. Aditya Birla Finance Ltd., the NCLAT held that the Corporate Insolvency Resolution Process (CIRP) can be initiated against a coborrower who has equal and similar liabilities with that of the primary borrower. The tribunal also rejected the argument that the loan documents were not properly stamped, stating that insufficient stamping is only a technical deficiency that can be rectified. The NCLAT further held that the declaration of a loan as NonPerforming Assets (NPA) under the Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act (SARFAESI Act) is an independent proceeding that cannot obstruct the financial creditor from initiating CIRP against the corporate debtor under the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC). The tribunal concluded that the corporate debtor cannot now refuse to repay the loan amount. The case is a significant ruling on the liability of coborrowers under the IBC.
