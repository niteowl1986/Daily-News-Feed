Image not found.IDFC FIRST Bank offers real-time tracking for overseas money transfers - APAC News Network

Published Date: Thu, 07 Nov 2024

IDFC FIRST Bank has introduced real-time tracking for international money transfers, allowing customers to monitor transactions from start to finish. The bank has partnered with SWIFT to provide this feature through its mobile app and internet banking. This update makes IDFC FIRST the first Indian bank to go live with SWIFT's Global Payments Innovation (GPI) via APIs in South Asia. The tracking tool provides customers with a clear view of their transfers, alerting them to any issues or errors. The feature is available 24/7 at no extra cost and covers transfers under the Reserve Bank of India's Liberalised Remittance Scheme. The bank's Head of Retail Liabilities, Chinmay Dhoble, highlighted that this feature aims to make cross-border transfers easier and more transparent for customers.

Image not found.Who are the top players in health insurance? - Cafemutual

Published Date: Thu, 07 Nov 2024

The General Insurance Council has released data showing that health insurance companies received a gross premium of Rs.59,600 crore in the first six months of FY 2025, a 9% growth from the previous year. The New India Assurance remains the market leader with a total premium collection of Rs 10,100 crore, accounting for 17% of the market share. Star Health & Allied Insurance and Oriental Insurance Company rank second and third, respectively. In the retail health segment, Star Health & Allied Insurance tops the list with a gross premium of Rs.7000 crore, followed by Care Health and Niva Bupa Health. The group health segment grew by 11%, with The New India Assurance dominating the segment. The overseas medical segment recorded a 14% growth, with Tata AIG General Insurance, ICICI Lombard General Insurance, and Bajaj Alliance General Insurance ranking as the top three players.

IFC to Invest USD 400 Million in Bajaj Finance to Boost Climate Finance - Energetica India

Published Date: Thu, 07 Nov 2024

The International Finance Corporation (IFC) has invested USD 400 million in Bajaj Finance Ltd. (BFL) to expand climate finance for electric vehicles and energy-efficient consumer goods in India. This investment aims to contribute to India's net-zero goals and promote financial inclusion by increasing access to green financing. BFL plans to increase its outstanding climate loans from USD 150 million to USD 600 million by 2027, making electric vehicles and energy-efficient products more accessible to consumers. The IFC's investment is expected to boost the competitiveness of the climate finance sector and support India's national climate goals. The partnership will also promote a shift towards sustainable consumer habits and provide more financing options for energy-efficient goods. This investment is crucial for India to achieve its net-zero goals, as the country faces a significant challenge in reducing its carbon emissions while meeting growing energy demands.
