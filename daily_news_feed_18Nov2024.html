FICO Survey: Brits Call for Stronger Protection as One in Four Report Scam Losses - sharewise

Published Date: Mon, 18 Nov 2024

A new study by FICO highlights the need for banks to balance customer satisfaction and fraud mitigation as real-time payments (RTP) become more popular. The study found that 26% of UK consumers have reported actual or suspected scam losses to their bank. Despite this, 79% of UK consumers have sent and received RTP, and 28% plan to increase their usage in the next 12 months. However, RTP-related scams, such as Authorised Push Payment (APP) fraud, are on the rise. FICO's research found that over half of consumers feel they are responsible if they fall for a scam, and 73% of consumers think banks should refund scam victims most of the time or always. To mitigate fraud, banks should focus on identifying and intervening in scam transactions, and provide customers with the tools, education, and automated failsafes to prevent scam losses.

Published Date: Mon, 18 Nov 2024

A FICO survey of 12,000 consumers across 14 countries highlights a surge in UK payment scams, with 26% reporting losses and 41% knowing someone affected. The widespread use of real-time payments (RTP) in the UK (79% send, 73% receive) exacerbates the problem. Despite this, a significant portion of consumers (54%) believe they are responsible for falling victim to scams, while 73% expect bank refunds. The survey strongly suggests that banks need to improve fraud detection systems (prioritized by 58% of respondents) to balance customer satisfaction with fraud mitigation. The increasing reliance on RTP necessitates better fraud prevention strategies and secure communication channels, like banking apps, to protect consumers. FICO emphasizes the need for banks to proactively combat scams, particularly through advanced fraud detection technologies and improved customer communication.

Published Date: Mon, 18 Nov 2024

DBS Bank and UnionPay have launched the UnionPay MultiCurrency Diamond debit card, which allows cardholders to make direct payments in 14 currencies. The card eliminates multicurrency conversion fees and supports currencies such as the US dollar, euro, and British pound. The physical card is made from environmentally friendly materials, and the product is fully digitalized, allowing customers to apply online. The card also offers additional features such as payment notifications, instant messaging, free travel insurance, and enhanced security measures. The UnionPay MultiCurrency Diamond debit card is a convenient and cost-effective way for cardholders to make international transactions.

Banks’ non-interest income gets a boost from listing gains - The Financial Express

Published Date: Mon, 18 Nov 2024

Recent initial public offerings (IPOs) have generated significant profits for banks, contributing to their noninterest income. Banks have aggressively invested in these IPOs, resulting in a 2551% increase in noninterest income for some major banks. The surge in listing gains has been a key driver of noninterest income, with some IPOs delivering returns of over 60%. For example, Waaree Energies shares listed at a 69% premium over the IPO allotment price, while Premier Energies and Bajaj Housing Finance saw returns of 120% and 114%, respectively. Most banks, except for a few conservative ones, actively invest in IPOs to maximize returns, and profits generated from these sales are categorized as noninterest income. The trend is expected to continue, with 32 public issues launched between April and October this year delivering listing day returns ranging from 10% to 120%.
