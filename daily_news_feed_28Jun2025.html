Image not found.Pulte: FHFA reviewing credit bureau policies - Scotsman Guide

Published Date: Fri, 27 Jun 2025

The Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA) is conducting a comprehensive review of all credit bureaus, as announced by FHFA Director Bill Pulte. This decision follows public criticism regarding the credit bureaus' power and lack of consumer remedies. Pulte's review stems from concerns about rising costs associated with credit pulls, particularly from Fair Isaac Corp. (FICO), which uses data from the three major credit bureaus (Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion) to calculate credit scores. Pulte previously expressed dissatisfaction with FICO's price hikes and hinted at potential agency action. Advocates, such as Brendan McKay from the Broker Action Coalition, have welcomed Pulte's scrutiny of FICO and urged him to extend the review to the credit bureaus themselves. The scope and implications of the FHFA's review remain unclear.

Image not found.DBS Bank India waives prepayment, foreclosure charges for registered MSMEs - The Hindu

Published Date: Fri, 27 Jun 2025

DBS Bank India has waived prepayment and foreclosure charges for all Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSME) borrowers registered with UDYAM. This move aims to enhance borrower flexibility and aligns with regulatory guidelines promoting transparency in lending. The waiver applies to both new and existing active borrowers, effective immediately. This decision addresses a common challenge faced by MSMEs, where foreclosure charges often discouraged early repayment or refinancing, hindering their cash flow management. DBS Bank India believes that access to flexible credit is crucial for MSMEs, and this waiver will enable businesses to optimize their finances by repaying loans early without penalty. The bank hopes this change will empower businesses to allocate funds towards growth opportunities and critical areas like inventory, technology, and expansion.

Image not found.DBS Bank India waives loan prepayment, foreclosure charges for MSMEs - Zee Business

Published Date: Fri, 27 Jun 2025

DBS Bank India (DBIL) announced the elimination of prepayment and foreclosure fees for loans taken by Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs). This decision, effective for both new and existing borrowers registered on the UDYAM portal, makes DBS one of the first banks in India to offer this benefit. The move aims to enhance MSME financial flexibility by allowing businesses to repay loans early without penalty and allocate funds to operational needs. Sudarshan Chari, Managing Director and Head of SME Banking at DBS Bank India, emphasized the importance of accessible and affordable credit for MSMEs, stating that the waiver addresses a common challenge hindering financial agility. This initiative aligns with broader regulatory efforts to promote transparency and borrower-centric lending practices. DBS Bank India continues to expand its MSME-focused services, including online account opening, quick business loans, foreign exchange services, and API-based banking solutions.

UGRO Capital, a technology-enabled MSME lender, emphasizes the importance of understanding businesses beyond their financial data, particularly in India's diverse and informal MSME sector. Image not found.Dutch investors pull back from U.S. stocks amid policy concerns: Rabobank survey - bastillepost.com

Published Date: Fri, 27 Jun 2025

A recent Rabobank survey reveals that over 25% of Dutch investors have reduced their U.S. stock holdings in the past six months. This shift is primarily driven by concerns over unpredictable U.S. policies, with investors characterizing the U.S. market climate as uncertain and unreliable. Nearly 60% of respondents expressed reluctance to invest further in U.S. equities, highlighting a widespread hesitation. The survey also found that investors perceive the U.S.'s role on the world stage as the biggest threat to their investments over the next year. These findings are based on a nationwide survey of 1,868 Dutch citizens conducted between April 14 and May 6, 2025. The survey underscores growing investor apprehension towards the U.S. market due to perceived policy instability.

Image not found.Sterling keeps climbing on struggling dollar - TradingView

Published Date: Fri, 27 Jun 2025

The pound sterling is experiencing its largest weekly gain against the US dollar in nearly four months, reaching a near four-year high. This surge is primarily attributed to the dollar's weakness rather than significant strength in the pound. Analysts suggest the pound's rise against the dollar is influenced by the euro's strength and the limited trading range of EURGBP. The Bank of England's slower pace of interest rate cuts compared to other central banks, particularly the European Central Bank, has provided domestic support for the pound. This is due to persistent inflation in the UK, which makes rate cuts less likely. Market observers are closely monitoring the UK's political landscape, considering the country's high debt-to-GDP ratio and current account deficit. Recent political developments, such as Prime Minister Starmer's scaling back of planned welfare cuts, are being assessed for their potential impact on the pound.

Image not found.NFO Alert: Introducing Bajaj Finserv Small Cap Fund - TheWire

Published Date: Fri, 27 Jun 2025

Bajaj Finserv Asset Management Company (AMC) has launched the Bajaj Finserv Small Cap Fund, a new fund offer (NFO) open from June 27th to July 11th, 2025. The fund aims to capitalize on the growth potential of small-cap companies while mitigating risk through a three-pronged strategy focusing on quality, growth, and value stocks. Bajaj Finserv AMC emphasizes the fund's differentiated investment process, leveraging the proprietary INQUBE framework, which combines top-down megatrends analysis, bottom-up stock selection, and behavioral insights. The fund's investment team boasts extensive multi-sector expertise and a proven track record of managing small-cap investments. Investors can participate in the NFO through online or offline channels, directly or through a mutual fund distributor. Bajaj Finserv AMC encourages investors to consider a systematic investment plan (SIP) to build long-term exposure to the small-cap sector and benefit from rupee-cost averaging.
