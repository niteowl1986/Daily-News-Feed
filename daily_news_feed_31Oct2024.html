Image not found.Stocks Under F&O Ban: Indiamart Intermesh, L&T Finance, IDFC First Bank, and Others - EquityPandit

Published Date: Wed, 30 Oct 2024

The National Stock Exchange (NSE) banned six stocks from futures and options (F&O) trade on October 30 due to high open interest. The banned stocks are IDFC First Bank, Indiamart Intermesh, L&T Finance, Manappuram Finance, Punjab National Bank, and RBL Bank. The ban is in place until the open interest falls below 80% of the marketwide position limit (MWPL). Traders who buy or sell these securities will be penalized, but they can still trade in the cash market. The NSE updates the F&O ban list daily, serving as a guide for traders and investors. The ban does not affect traders who trade in indices.

Image not found.The DECODE Webinar: Leveraging Cloud to Break Data Silos for Seamless Integration - AIM

Published Date: Wed, 30 Oct 2024

Data silos are a significant challenge for organizations, with nearly 85% of enterprises seeing them as a major obstacle to digital transformation. The DECODE webinar, hosted by DBS Bank and AIM Media House, explored the causes and impact of data silos, as well as the role of cloud technology in overcoming this challenge. Data silos arise when systems are not designed to integrate or communicate effectively, leading to inefficiencies and incomplete views of business operations. Cloud integration is a key solution to breaking down data silos, providing an end-to-end data lifecycle management solution and facilitating real-time data access. Effective data governance is also crucial, involving a comprehensive framework that defines roles, responsibilities, and policies for data management and sharing. The success of data integration efforts can be measured in various dimensions, including cost savings, performance improvements, and user satisfaction.

HDFC Standard Life Insurance Company Ltd acquired shares of PNB Housing Finance worth Rs 90 crore through an open market transaction. This acquisition marks a notable move for HDFC Standard Life as PNB Housing Finance sees changes in its shareholder base. Meanwhile, foreign investor Asia Opportunities V (Mauritius) Ltd reduced its stake in PNB Housing Finance by selling shares at the same price. This sale brought Asia Opportunities V's stake down to 2.5% from 3.5%. The transaction is part of Asia Opportunities V's ongoing stake adjustments in PNB Housing Finance, having sold a 3% stake in August and a cumulative 4% stake in May.
