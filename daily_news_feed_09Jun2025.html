Image not found.Home loan rates fall after RBI rate cut; existing borrowers benefit more - Mathrubhumi English

Published Date: Mon, 09 Jun 2025

Following the RBI's 50 basis point repo rate cut, four major public sector banks have lowered their lending rates. Bank of Baroda, Punjab National Bank, Bank of India, and UCO Bank have adjusted their repo-linked lending rates (RLLR) and/or Marginal Cost of Funds based Lending Rates (MCLR) to reflect the central bank's easing stance. HDFC Bank, a leading private sector lender, also reduced its MCLR by 10 basis points across tenures. Existing borrowers with floating-rate loans will automatically benefit from the lower interest rates, while new borrowers may not receive the full benefit due to banks adjusting spreads. To maintain profitability, lenders are also expected to reduce returns on fixed deposits (FDs). The RBI's actions aim to stimulate credit growth and support economic activity.

Published Date: Mon, 09 Jun 2025

Elliott Investment Management is urging Sumitomo Realty & Development Co., Ltd. to implement significant changes to improve corporate governance and shareholder value. Elliott, a major shareholder, criticizes Sumitomo Realty's undervaluation, citing excessive cross-shareholdings, declining capital efficiency, and subpar governance. Elliott calls for increased shareholder payouts, a reduction in cross-shareholdings, a clear return target, and enhanced governance practices. They threaten to vote against senior management's reappointment at the 2025 AGM unless these changes are made. Elliott highlights Sumitomo Realty's poor performance compared to peers, citing low approval ratings for the Chairman and a lack of progress on previous recommendations. They argue that unwinding cross-shareholdings and improving capital efficiency would unlock significant value for shareholders. Elliott emphasizes the urgency of these changes, stating that Sumitomo Realty's market undervaluation is a result of self-imposed issues that can be addressed quickly. They urge shareholders to engage with management and vote accordingly at the upcoming AGM. Elliott's actions highlight the growing pressure on Japanese companies to improve shareholder returns and governance practices.
