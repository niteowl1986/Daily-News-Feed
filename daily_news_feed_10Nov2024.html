Image not found.Singapore’s DBS eyes Malaysian bank stakes in expansion push, sources say - Selangor Journal

Published Date: Sat, 09 Nov 2024

DBS Group Holdings, Singapore's largest lender, is exploring expansion into Malaysia through potential acquisitions of stakes in local banks. The bank is considering purchasing a 40% stake in Alliance Bank Malaysia, valued at around $460 million, currently owned by Temasek, Singapore's state investor. Other options include buying Kuwait Finance House's Malaysian retail banking assets, worth over $500 million. Any acquisition would require approval from Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM). DBS is the only Singaporean bank without a retail presence in Malaysia, and the expansion plan comes amid improving economic prospects for the country. The bank's deputy CEO, Tan Su Shan, is set to take over as CEO in March next year, making her the first woman to lead the bank.

News: RBI imposes monetary penalty on South Indian Bank - PSU Connect

Published Date: Sat, 09 Nov 2024

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has imposed a penalty of Rs 1 lakh on South Indian Bank for non-compliance with certain directions. A statutory inspection was conducted to evaluate the bank's financial position as of March 31, 2023. The RBI found that the bank failed to notify certain customers about penal charges for non-maintenance of minimum balance. Additionally, the bank marked lien against certain NRE savings deposit accounts without proper notification. The penalty is based on deficiencies in statutory and regulatory compliance and does not affect the validity of any transactions or agreements with customers. The RBI issued a notice to the bank, which replied and made oral submissions during a personal hearing, leading to the imposition of the penalty.
