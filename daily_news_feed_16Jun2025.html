Image not found.Ahmedabad plane crash: Insurers asked to expedite payment of insurance claims for deceased - Insurance Khabar

Published Date: Sun, 15 Jun 2025

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) has instructed insurance companies to expedite the processing and payment of claims related to the Air India plane crash. IRDAI has waived procedural formalities and directed companies not to delay or reject claims based on passenger lists. To streamline the process, IRDAI has empowered insurance companies to obtain necessary information directly from relevant authorities, eliminating the need for FIRs and postmortem reports. A special cell has been established near the hospital treating victims, with each insurance company appointing a nodal officer to collaborate on claim resolution. Major life insurance companies, including LIC, Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance, and HDFC Life, have already initiated claim processing and established dedicated teams to prioritize payments for death or disability claims. IRDAI has also mandated weekly reports from insurance companies for the next month to monitor progress and ensure timely claim settlements.

Image not found.Downside risks to dairy emerging later this year – Rabobank - farmersweekly.co.nz

Published Date: Mon, 16 Jun 2025

Rabobank's latest Dairy Quarterly report highlights increasing downside risks to the global dairy outlook in the second half of 2023. While production growth in the first half of the year supported dairy prices, a projected surge in production in the coming quarters, driven by the US, EU, and South America, may lead to a price correction. Demand concerns are also emerging, with weak consumer confidence in the US, economic struggles in China, and declining sales in various regions. Despite these risks, dairy product prices, particularly in Oceania, have reached multi-year highs, with Fonterra forecasting a record-high milk price for the 2025-2026 season. However, Fonterra acknowledges the uncertain macroeconomic backdrop and the potential for market volatility. Rabobank expects a 2% increase in dairy collections for the 2025-2026 season, approaching record volumes.

Image not found.Grains and oilseeds trade continues to grow, despite global upheaval – Rabobank World Map - medianet.com.au

Published Date: Sun, 15 Jun 2025

The global grains and oilseeds trade reached 880 million metric tons in 2023-24, valued at USD 330 billion, according to Rabobank's latest report. Despite the war in Ukraine's impact on prices, trade volumes have grown steadily over the past five years, though at a slower pace than the previous decade. Wheat remains the most traded grain, with Russia emerging as the leading exporter, surpassing competitors like Ukraine, the EU, and Australia. Soybeans are the most traded oilseed, with production concentrated in Brazil, the US, and South America, while Asia and the EU are major importers. Looking ahead, Rabobank anticipates growing demand for wheat in Sub-Saharan Africa and potential slowdown in soybean imports by China. Factors like biofuel policies, the EU Deforestation Regulation, and weather patterns will also influence future trade flows.
