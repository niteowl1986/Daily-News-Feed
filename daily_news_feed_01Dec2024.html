Image not found.EPFO Orders Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance To Pay ₹121 Crore In Default Case - Local Haryana

Published Date: Sat, 30 Nov 2024

The Employees' Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO) has ordered Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance to pay Rs 121 crore for defaulting on provident fund contributions. The company had manipulated salary components to evade contributions, a practice deemed unacceptable by the EPFO. The EPFO found that Bajaj Allianz had failed to remit provident fund dues on various allowances from September 2014 to August 2019. The company had split salary components into different perks to avoid paying the necessary provident fund dues. The EPFO has mandated that the company rectify this situation by paying the owed amount within 60 days. This directive has significant financial implications for Bajaj Allianz and may impact its financial statements and operational budget, serving as a reminder to all organizations about their responsibilities towards their employees' financial security.

RBL Bank Ends Co-Brand Credit Card Partnership with Bajaj Finance - Bizz Buzz

Published Date: Sat, 30 Nov 2024

RBL Bank has ended its cobrand credit card partnership with Bajaj Finance, focusing on new partnerships and seamless service for existing cards. The bank will continue to service its existing portfolio of approximately 1 million cobranded credit cards, with customers retaining their current benefits. The decision aligns with RBL Bank's evolving credit card business strategy, which focuses on diversifying its portfolio through direct channels and new cobrand partnerships. The bank has expanded its issuance capabilities and reduced its reliance on the Bajaj Finance partnership, scaling down from issuing 1.5 lakh cards in September 2023 to just 37,000 in September 2024. RBL Bank is focused on leveraging its credit card customer base for cross-selling opportunities and has reported a 17% year-on-year increase in credit card advances. The bank's credit card advances stood at ₹17,538 crore as of Q2 FY25, showcasing its robust presence in the credit card market.
