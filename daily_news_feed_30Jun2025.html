Image not found.DBS Bank Account Holders Alert: 6% Charge for Not Maintaining Minimum Balance - Times Bull

Published Date: Sun, 29 Jun 2025

DBS Bank India, a subsidiary of Singapore's DBS, will implement a new average monthly balance requirement for its various savings accounts starting August 1, 2025. The minimum average monthly balance varies by account type, ranging from Rs 1,000 for the DBS Bank Savings Account to Rs 10,000 for the DBS Bank Savings Account and TASC Savings Youth Power Account. Customers who fail to maintain the required average balance will face a penalty of up to 6% on the shortfall. For example, an account holder with Rs 8,500 in their DBS Bank Savings Account would incur a 6% penalty on the Rs 1,500 shortfall from the Rs 10,000 minimum. DBS Bank has notified its customers about this new rule via text messages. This change is expected to impact a significant number of DBS Bank account holders in India.

Image not found.Australian beef production volumes balanced with global demand - Young Witness

Published Date: Sun, 29 Jun 2025

Rabobank's Australian Beef Seasonal Outlook 2025 predicts continued high beef production volumes in Australia, nearing the record set in 2024. This high production is expected to be supported by growing global demand, leading to stable prices and good returns for Australian beef producers. While Australia's domestic beef consumption is projected to slightly decrease due to economic pressures, exports are expected to remain strong, accounting for 75% of production. The US market is anticipated to remain the largest for Australian beef, driving import demand and higher prices. Global beef production is projected to contract slightly in 2025, with declines in Brazil, the US, and China, which will support the Australian cattle market. Australian cattle prices are expected to remain steady with some upside potential in 2025, driven by global demand and high production volumes. RaboResearch forecasts the National Young Cattle Indicator to trade between 360ckg and 425ckg lwt in 2025, representing a 23% increase from the average price of 2024. Despite potential dry conditions in 2026, beef producer confidence remains good for 2025, with higher cash receipts expected to offset rising costs.

Image not found.Hackers Steal Rs 1.9 Crore from Aditya Birla Capital App, 435 Customers Impacted - TechStory

Published Date: Sun, 29 Jun 2025

Aditya Birla Capital Digital Limited (ABCD) suffered a major cyber fraud where hackers illicitly sold approximately Rs. crore worth of digital gold from 435 customer accounts. The breach involved manipulation of the app's API, bypassing OTP verification, and transferring proceeds to personal bank accounts. ABCD detected the breach on June 9, 2025, after a surge in customer complaints and immediately suspended the digital gold selling feature. The company restored affected digital gold holdings, froze suspicious transactions, and strengthened its cybersecurity framework with the help of law enforcement and cyber insurance partners. Shares of Aditya Birla Capital Ltd. initially dipped but recovered, reflecting the company's swift response and transparency. The incident highlights the vulnerability of digital platforms handling financial transactions and underscores the need for robust cybersecurity measures and consumer awareness.
