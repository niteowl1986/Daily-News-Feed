Image not found.UGRO Capital receives patent for its proprietary credit scoring model - BusinessLine

Published Date: Wed, 11 Dec 2024

UGRO Capital has received a patent from the government's patent office for its proprietary credit scoring model, GRO Score. The model, in its third iteration, uses alternate data such as repayment histories, banking transactions, and GST records to assess borrowers without formal income documentation. GRO Score has assessed over 1 lakh loan applications, analyzing over 1 lakh bureau records, 1 lakh bank statements, and 80,000 GST reports, unlocking capital for MSMEs. Shachindra Nath, Founder and Managing Director of UGRO Capital, said that GRO Score provides nuanced risk assessments tailored to the unique challenges of MSME borrowers, creating opportunities for growth and resilience. The company's credit scoring model is designed to provide a comprehensive assessment of borrowers without formal income documentation. This innovation is expected to unlock capital for MSMEs and provide opportunities for growth and resilience.

MPOWER Financing Advocates for Enhancements to Canada's Study Permit Program - IT News Online

Published Date: Wed, 11 Dec 2024

MPOWER Financing, a fintech firm, submitted a report to the Standing Committee on Immigration and Citizenship analyzing recent reforms to Canada's study permit program. The report found that while the reforms aimed to strengthen program credibility and combat fraud, they have inadvertently harmed graduate-level education and failed to diversify the student population. MPOWER recommended piloting advanced financial verification programs for prospective students to support the government's objectives. The company's internal data showed that students from Nigeria, Ghana, and Kenya with MPOWER loans had higher study permit approval rates, highlighting the role financiers can play in facilitating diversity in international education. MPOWER suggested enhancing financial verification processes and promoting targeted recruitment efforts to promote diversity and inclusion in Canadian higher education. The full submission is available on OurCommons.ca, and MPOWER's detailed analysis can be found on the MPOWERed Insights blog.
