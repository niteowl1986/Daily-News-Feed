Published Date: Fri, 01 Nov 2024

Indian banks' loans rose 12% in the two weeks to October 18 compared to the same period last year, according to the Reserve Bank of India's weekly statistical supplement. Outstanding loans fell by 1 trillion rupees ($130 billion) to 1.3 trillion rupees. Non-food credit decreased by 1 trillion rupees to 1.2 trillion rupees, while food credit increased by 100 billion rupees to 200 billion rupees. Bank deposits fell by 1 trillion rupees to 1.2 trillion rupees in the same period. The RBI's data also showed that Indian banks' deposits rose 11% year-on-year. The Reserve Bank of India has not implemented a rate cut at this stage, according to the RBI Governor.
