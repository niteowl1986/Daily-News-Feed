Published Date: Thu, 03 Jul 2025

Global Banking & Finance Review published an interview with Anurag Mohapatra, SME and Director of Fraud Strategy at NiCE Actimize, exploring scam response strategies in Asia. Mohapatra found a diverse landscape, with some countries like Thailand holding multiple sectors accountable for scams, while others like Vietnam and Indonesia primarily focus on blocking scam messages and calls. He predicts Asia will adopt a mixed approach, leaning towards shared responsibility and prevention measures, similar to the UK and Australia, respectively. Mohapatra emphasizes the need for regional collaboration and data sharing to combat evolving scam tactics. He highlights the inadequacy of current systems that assign minimal liability to banks for authorized scam payments, calling for a shift towards shared accountability to protect consumer trust. Mohapatra stresses the importance of real-time fraud intelligence sharing across institutions to effectively counter sophisticated scams. He believes a networked defense approach, exemplified by initiatives like Australia's National AntiScam Centre, is crucial for staying ahead of scammers.
