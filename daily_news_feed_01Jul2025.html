Image not found.BNPL Coming to FICO Credit Scores Later this Year | Personal Loans and Advice - US News Money

Published Date: Mon, 30 Jun 2025

FICO, the leading credit scoring model, will introduce two new scores this fall that incorporate buy now, pay later (BNPL) data. These scores, FICO Score 10 BNPL and FICO Score 10 T BNPL, will treat BNPL activity differently from traditional credit products, aggregating transactions within a timeframe rather than counting them individually. While BNPL usage is currently not factored into credit scores, FICO's move signals a potential shift in the industry, with Affirm already reporting some BNPL activity to credit bureaus. The new scores aim to provide lenders with a more comprehensive view of consumer creditworthiness, potentially impacting lending decisions. Responsible BNPL use could help consumers build credit, particularly younger generations who frequently utilize these services. Conversely, overspending or late payments could negatively affect credit scores.

Image not found.CAM acts on UGRO Capital's ₹1,400 crore acquisition of Profectus Capital - Bar and Bench

Published Date: Mon, 30 Jun 2025

URGO Capital, a data-driven MSME lending platform, is acquiring Profectus Capital Pvt. Ltd. from Actis, a UK private equity firm, for ₹1,400 crore in an all-cash deal. This acquisition will make Profectus a wholly-owned subsidiary of UGRO and significantly expand its asset under management (AUM) to approximately ₹15,500 crore. The deal is funded through UGRO's recent equity raise and upcoming CCD issuances, strengthening its position in secured lending segments like LAP, machinery, supply chain, and school financing. Profectus brings a robust loan book with low NPAs and a network of 28 branches across seven states, contributing to UGRO's projected annual profit increase of ₹150 crore and cost synergies of ₹115 crore. The acquisition is expected to enhance UGRO's return on assets by 60-70 basis points. Subject to regulatory and shareholder approvals, including from the Reserve Bank of India, the deal is anticipated to close in the coming months.

Image not found.Yes Bank sale opens door to more Asian investment in Indian banks - TheBanker.com

Published Date: Mon, 30 Jun 2025

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has granted Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corporation (SMBC) in-principle approval to acquire a 20% stake in Yes Bank, marking a potential shift towards increased foreign ownership in India's banking sector. This move comes as Indian banks seek significant capital to support the country's economic growth, attracting interest from Asian and Middle Eastern lenders. SMBC's acquisition, subject to final approvals, will see it acquire existing shareholdings of domestic banks in Yes Bank, who invested capital in the lender following its 2020 financial difficulties. The RBI views this development as beneficial, potentially leading to knowledge sharing and best practices between international and Indian banks, enhancing industry oversight. This development follows a trend of foreign banks expanding their presence in India, exemplified by DBS's acquisition of Lakshmi Vilas Bank in 2020. Experts anticipate further interest from South Korean, Taiwanese, and Middle Eastern banks seeking opportunities in India's high-growth banking sector.

Image not found.AIIB invests ₹1,301 crore in Aditya Birla Capital for green infrastructure - BusinessLine

Published Date: Mon, 30 Jun 2025

The Asian Infrastructure Investment Bank (AIIB) has invested ₹1,000 crore in Aditya Birla Capital Limited (ABCL) through a three-year private placement of Non-Convertible Debentures.
