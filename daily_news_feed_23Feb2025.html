Chinese New Year Bank Notes 2025, Check OCBC, DBS, My Bank Reservation & Notes Collection Dates - Pasteur Institute of India

Published Date: Sat, 22 Feb 2025

Major Singaporean banks, including DBS, OCBC, and My Bank, are implementing various measures to facilitate the traditional Chinese New Year (CNY) practice of giving new notes. These measures include online reservation systems, expanded ATM services, and digital gifting options like DBS's QR Gift and OCBC's eAng Bao. To enhance convenience and reduce waiting times, DBS has doubled its online reservation slots compared to last year. In addition to traditional new notes, banks are actively promoting the use of "fit notes," which are reusable, high-quality notes, as a sustainable alternative. Walk-in exchanges are also available at select branches, with priority given to senior citizens and individuals with disabilities. These initiatives aim to make CNY celebrations safer, easier, and more environmentally friendly for all.

Image not found.Bajaj Allianz Life goes live with BIMA-ASBA - BusinessLine

Published Date: Sat, 22 Feb 2025

Bajaj Allianz Life Insurance Company (BALIC) has become the first insurer to implement the BIMA Application Supported by Blocked Amount (BimaASBA) facility. This IRDAI initiative allows policyholders to authorize the blocking of a specified amount (up to ₹2 lakh) in their bank account via UPI for premium payments. The blocked amount is debited only after the insurer completes underwriting and accepts the proposal, ensuring policyholders retain access to their funds and potential interest earnings until confirmation. If the application is not processed within 14 days or rejected, the blocked amount is automatically released back to the customer. BALIC partnered with payment partners to launch this process, aiming to enhance operational ease, transparency, and customer convenience. This move addresses concerns about refunds in case a policy is not issued, strengthening customer trust and flexibility.

Image not found.Rabobank knew of profit motive in mask deal but took no action, investigation reveals - NL Times

Published Date: Sat, 22 Feb 2025

Rabobank was aware as early as July 2020 that profits from a controversial face mask deal involving Sywert van Lienden and his partners would not solely benefit charities. Despite this knowledge, the bank did not take action, according to an investigation by journalist platform Pointer. Internal recordings reveal that Rabobank was concerned about the trio's plan to use profits for personal expenses, including paying off debts and securing a comfortable life. The bank initially believed the operation was non-profit and raised red flags when it learned of millions in dividend payouts to the entrepreneurs. Rabobank accepted a "moral compass" document outlining the profit distribution as justification and continued its banking relationship with the group. The controversy resurfaced in 2022, leading Rabobank to claim that former executive Barbara Baarsma had been misled about the deal's financing. The bank has not publicly explained why it did not intervene despite its awareness of the profit distribution plan.
