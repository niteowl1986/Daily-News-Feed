Image not found.Byju's insolvency: Aditya Birla Finance, Glas Trust move NCLT to replace resolution professional - Bar & Bench - Indian Legal News

Published Date: Mon, 04 Nov 2024

Aditya Birla Finance and Glas Trust Company have filed applications with the National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) to replace the Resolution Professional (RP) of Think and Learn, Byju's parent company. The plea was mentioned for urgent hearing, and the NCLT agreed to list the case for hearing in the coming week. The RP had classified only one creditor as a financial creditor, giving that creditor 100% voting rights in the Committee of Creditors (CoC). Glas Trust and Aditya Birla Finance claim they are financial creditors and want to participate in the CoC meetings. The Karnataka High Court has stayed the conduct of CoC meetings and asked the NCLT to hear and decide these applications within two weeks. The Supreme Court had allowed Glas Trust's appeal challenging the NCLAT's decision to halt insolvency proceedings against Think & Learn, and the matter has been remitted back to NCLT to decide the next course of action.
