Image not found.RBI approves 6-month extension for MD & CEO of Yes Bank - The Statesman

Published Date: Fri, 13 Jun 2025

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has granted a six-month extension to Prashant Kumar's tenure as Managing Director and CEO of Yes Bank, effective October 6, 2025, or until a successor is appointed. This extension comes as Yes Bank initiates a global search for a new CEO. Kumar's initial three-year extension, granted in 2022, was set to expire in October 2025. Notably, Sumitomo Mitsui Banking Corp (SMBC) recently agreed to acquire a 20% stake in Yes Bank for Rs 13,483 crore. Kumar, a former SBI executive, was appointed CEO of Yes Bank following a bailout by a consortium of banks led by SBI in 2020. The RBI approved the extension application submitted by Yes Bank under the Banking Regulation Act, 1949.

Published Date: Fri, 13 Jun 2025

DBS Bank India has launched DBS Treasures, a premium banking and wealth management program tailored for high-net-worth individuals. The program offers personalized banking, wealth management, lifestyle, travel, and health privileges based on a tiered system linked to Total Relationship Value (TRV). TRV is calculated by aggregating average quarterly balances across various accounts, including savings, term deposits, insurance, investments, and loans. The program aims to elevate the banking experience for discerning clients seeking personalized financial solutions and premium lifestyle benefits.

Image not found.Toyota Kirloskar Motor Launches 3-Month EMI Holiday Scheme for Glanza and Hyryder Models - Autocar Professional

Published Date: Fri, 13 Jun 2025

Toyota Kirloskar Motor (TKM) has launched a limited-time financing scheme called "Buy Now, Pay in Navratri" for its Glanza and Urban Cruiser Hyryder models. The scheme, in partnership with Toyota Financial Services, allows customers to purchase vehicles immediately and defer EMI payments for three months until the Navratri festival. During this period, customers pay a nominal EMI of Rs. 99. TKM aims to provide customers with flexibility in their vehicle purchase timing and payment schedules, aligning EMI commencement with the festive spending period. Interested customers are advised to visit authorized dealerships for detailed terms and conditions and complete applications before the June 30 deadline.

Image not found.Mutual funds go bargain hunting in May, buy Rs 29,000 crore of shares from exiting promoters, FIIs - Moneycontrol

Published Date: Fri, 13 Jun 2025

In May, mutual funds (MFs) emerged as significant buyers in the Indian stock market, investing over Rs 29,000 crore through block deals amidst heavy stake sales by promoters and foreign institutional investors. Total block and bulk deal activity reached Rs 50,000 crore, the highest in nine months, driven by MFs' active participation. Prominent transactions included ITC, where MFs bought shares worth Rs 7,880 crore after British American Tobacco's stake sale, and IndiGo, where MFs invested Rs 4,900 crore following Rakesh Gangwal's exit. MFs also invested heavily in Bharti Airtel after Singtel reduced its stake. Analysts attribute this buying spree to MFs' substantial cash reserves and the opportunity to invest in quality companies at attractive valuations during large block deals. The surge in MF investments reflects the market's maturity and depth, as it efficiently absorbs large-scale transactions.
