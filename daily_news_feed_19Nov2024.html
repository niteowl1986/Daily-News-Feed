FICO Survey: Brits Call for Stronger Protection as One in Four Report Scam Losses - sharewise

Published Date: Mon, 18 Nov 2024

A new study by FICO highlights the need for banks to balance customer satisfaction with fraud mitigation as real-time payments (RTP) surge in popularity. The survey found that 26% of UK consumers have reported actual or suspected scam losses to their bank. Despite the rise of RTP, scams are following, with 41% of respondents saying their friends or family members have been scammed. The study suggests that financial institutions should focus on mitigating fraud at the front end by deploying better fraud detection systems. Consumers expect their banks to provide the tools, education, and automated failsafes to help prevent scam losses. FICO's Customer Communications Service Scam Signal, which uses telephony signals to detect potential scams, received the AntiFraud Solution award at the Credit Collections Technology Awards.

Image not found.DTB, Airtel Money and Credable enter tripartite to promote financial inclusion - New Vision

Published Date: Mon, 18 Nov 2024

Diamond Trust Bank (DTB) Uganda, Credable Group, and Airtel Money have signed a tripartite agreement to launch products that increase financial inclusion in Uganda. The agreement comes with the launch of Kwasa Kwasa, a mobile-based consumer banking solution that offers Airtel Money customers in Uganda short-term loans. Kwasa Kwasa allows customers to borrow from as low as sh5,000 to sh2m without collateral, and the loan repayment period is 30 days with flexibility to rollover. The product aims to bridge gaps in accessibility for individuals and small businesses who often find traditional banking loans difficult to obtain. The partners are committed to ensuring a fast, reliable, and exceptional user experience for Kwasa Kwasa customers. In its pilot stage, DTB lent out sh32bn to over 50,000 customers with a loan compliance repayment rate of 64%.
