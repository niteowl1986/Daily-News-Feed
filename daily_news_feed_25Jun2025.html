Image not found.'Under pressure': NZ farms face succession challenges - NZ Herald

Published Date: Tue, 24 Jun 2025

A new Rabobank report, "Changing of the Guard," highlights the impending massive transfer of wealth in New Zealand's agricultural sector. Over half of the country's 17,000+ farm and orchard owners will reach retirement age within the next decade, leading to an estimated $150 billion transfer of farm assets. The report, released at the Primary Industries New Zealand Summit, found that only a third of farmers have a formal succession plan in place, with many facing challenges such as generational disconnect and financial obstacles. Experts emphasize the need for early planning and open communication within families to ensure a smooth transition. Innovative models like partial sales, transferring ownership to iwi or whanau, and engaging private capital are emerging to address the succession challenge. The report underscores the urgency for farmers to proactively address succession planning to secure the future of their land and legacy.

Image not found.Beyond the bottom line: ESG as a strategic imperative in private markets - Davy

Published Date: Tue, 24 Jun 2025

ESG factors are increasingly influencing private market investments, moving beyond compliance to become a strategic driver. Infrastructure and real estate sectors are leading this shift, with investors prioritizing sustainability, resilience, and social impact alongside financial returns. ESG integration in infrastructure is evident in investments in renewables, green transport, and digital infrastructure, while in real estate, it's driving demand for energy-efficient buildings and tenant-focused amenities. The integration of ESG considerations is leading to tangible benefits, including improved asset performance, tenant retention, and access to favorable financing. Furthermore, a "brown discount" is emerging for properties lacking ESG credentials, while those meeting high standards command a "green premium." This trend indicates a growing conviction among private market investors that ESG integration is not only responsible but also a pathway to long-term value creation.
