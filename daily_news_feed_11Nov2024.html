DBS Reportedly Eyeing Acquisition of Alliance Bank, KFH Assets for Malaysia Expansion - Fintech News Singapore

Published Date: Mon, 11 Nov 2024

DBS Group, Singapore's largest bank, is exploring acquisitions in Malaysia as part of its regional expansion strategy. Potential targets include a stake in Alliance Bank Malaysia held by Temasek, valued at US$460 million, and retail banking assets from Kuwait Finance House, worth over US$500 million. Both acquisitions would require approval from Malaysia's central bank, Bank Negara Malaysia. The discussions are still in preliminary stages, and no agreements have been reached. Malaysia's improving economic outlook has made it an attractive market for financial institutions, with DBS seeking to enter the market after a failed attempt in 2012. DBS has a history of targeted acquisitions, including the purchase of Citigroup's consumer banking operations in Taiwan last August, and is looking to expand its presence across Asia.

RBI imposes Rs 5900000 penalty on THIS bank, days after slapping Rs 1 crore fine on India's largest bank - DNA India

Published Date: Sun, 10 Nov 2024

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has imposed a penalty of Rs 5 million on South Indian Bank for non-compliance with certain directions. The bank was found to have levied penal charges for non-maintenance of minimum balance without notifying certain customers. Additionally, the bank marked lien against certain NRE savings deposit accounts. The RBI said the penalty is based on deficiencies in statutory and regulatory compliance and is not intended to pronounce upon the validity of any transaction or agreement entered into by the bank with its customers. The penalty is without prejudice to any other action that may be initiated by RBI against the bank. South Indian Bank is a major private sector bank headquartered in Thrissur, Kerala, with a market cap of Rs 6287 crore.

Image not found.Stocks Under F&O Ban: Manappuram Finance, Aditya Birla Fashion, and Others - EquityPandit

Published Date: Mon, 11 Nov 2024

The National Stock Exchange (NSE) banned three stocks from trade on November 11 due to high open market interest. The banned stocks are Aditya Birla Fashion and Retail Ltd, Granules India Ltd, and Manappuram Finance Ltd. The ban was imposed as the open interest for these securities exceeded 95% of the marketwide position limit (MWPL). The ban will be lifted once the position falls below 80%. Traders will face penalties for buying or selling these securities, but they can still trade in the cash market. The NSE updates the list of securities on the F&O ban list daily, serving as a guide for traders and investors.
