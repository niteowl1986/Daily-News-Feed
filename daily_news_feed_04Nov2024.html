Image not found.Private banks provisioning due to MFI stress on the rise - The Financial Express

Published Date: Sun, 03 Nov 2024

Private banks have increased provisions for loans to microfinance institutions (MFIs) due to rising stress in the sector. Banks like Kotak Mahindra, IDFC First, RBL, and IndusInd have made significant provisions for loans to MFIs. The Reserve Bank of India has directed banks and NBFCs to ensure borrowers fully repay outstanding loans before availing of new ones. Ashok Vaswani, MD and CEO at Kotak Mahindra Bank, said the bank is being cautious in the MFI space due to overleveraging and slowdown in the rural household economy. IDFC First Bank has made provisions of Rs 568 crore, including Rs 315 crore in the microfinance institution business. The microfinance portfolio as a percentage of overall loan book has reduced, leading to a slump in net profit for some banks.

Paxos Launches USDG in Singapore, Partnering with DBS for Dollar-Backed Stability - The Tech Report

Published Date: Sun, 03 Nov 2024

Paxos, a regulated blockchain company, has launched a new stablecoin called Global Dollar (USDG) in Singapore, which is backed by United States dollars and managed by DBS Bank. This development comes after Paxos launched its UAE-regulated interest-bearing stablecoin USDL five months ago. The company announced the launch on Twitter, stating that USDG is the sixth trusted digital asset from Paxos and its affiliates. The stablecoin meets the new guidelines set by Singapore's Monetary Authority (MAS) in August 2023, ensuring it is safe and reliable for users. Paxos plans to expand USDG beyond Ethereum, making it available on other blockchains as regulatory conditions shift. The company aims to drive the next wave of stablecoin adoption, catering to both crypto communities and regulated institutions.

Published Date: Sun, 03 Nov 2024

Bajaj Housing Finance's stock price has plummeted by 28% from its all-time high, trading below Rs 150. The decline has raised questions among investors about whether it's an opportune time to buy or exercise caution. The stock's fall can be attributed to market corrections and the end of the IPO lock-in period, which allowed early investors to sell their shares. Market analysts have expressed mixed sentiments, with some believing the current price levels present a buying opportunity and others recommending caution. The stock's decline has been characterized by a lack of strong buying momentum and bearish trends in technical indicators. Despite the recent downturn, Bajaj Housing Finance remains one of India's largest non-deposit-taking housing finance companies, with impressive growth metrics and a strong business model, making it an attractive option for long-term investors.
